Since 1946

In 1939, Armand Marquiset decided to devote his life to the poors. This project resulted in the creation of the association Les Petits Frères des Pauvres. His goal was to help the elderly with little resources. Love the poorest like brothers and help them as much as possible, that was what drove Armand Marquiset.

Nowadays, Les Petits Frères des Pauvres still live under theses values of respect and brotherhood. Volunteers and employees offer care and support on the long run in order for the persons to start over and build a relationship network while giving them the material support they need.

Following the intuition of their founder, Les Petits Frères des Pauvres are adapting theirs actions to the needs of our society and focusing on the new poverties : homeless old people, the seriously ill on their last days, isolated people living in difficult suburbs, …

Key Dates

1930/1939 Armand Marquiset start volunteering in social care at “La mie de pain”. On the 7th of July 1939, he received the revelation of the Petits Frères des Pauvres at Notre-Dame of Paris. The concept is born but the creation of the association is postpone due to WW2.
April 19th 1946 creation of the association and organisation in Paris of the first action towards the “old poors” of the neighborhood of Saint-Ambroise. 1949 : creation of the first “Chateau du bonheur” (castle of happiness), native house of Armand Marquiset, reorganized in order to welcome his protegé for the holidays.
1951 First Christmas eve
1965 Armand Marquiset leaves, the association take a less religious orientation
1981 Les Petits Frères des Pauvres are recognized as public utility.
2016 70th anniversary
2017 Opening of the first English fraternity “The Little Brothers”
